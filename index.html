OCBC Cycle Singapore

11 - 12 May 2019
Singapore Sports Hub

OCBC is organizing a cycling event for everyone on 11 and 12th of May.
There are competitive and a leisure classifications for the cyclers. The different categories of cyclers also include Adults, Kids & Families and Corporate cyclers.

To elaborate, there are competitive races for both adult and corporate racers.
There are 2 competitive races for corporate cyclers, one at 6:30 am on 11th May and the second one at 5:15 am on 12th May.

For adult cyclers there is one race on 11th May at 10 am.
There is a table provided below with details about the competitive and non-competitive races as well as their fees.
Furthermore there is an early bird discount for individuals who want to register early, however it will be ending very soon.

8 LITTLE STEPS FOR THE ENVIRONMENT

OCBC is also helping the environment in the following 8 ways:

  • 1) Plant 1 tree for every 50 cyclist.

  • 2) No single-use plastics will be consumed for the event-pack collection.

  • 3) Event bibs will be issued in wood-free paper envelopes.

  • 4) Event medals will be wrapped in tracing paper.

  • 5) Organise workshops on recycling and upcycling.

  • 6) Replace trophies for the OCBC Cycle Speedway Championships with reusable steel tumblers from Hydro Flask.

  • 7) Repurpose event banners and used plastic containers into useful items, such as tote bags and furniture.

  • 8) Compost fruit peels into plant fertilisers.

    Safety

    In order to ensure the safety of every individual during the event, OCBC has come up with several guidelines and precautions for everyone to follow. They ask that participants come an hour before the official start time of the ride. One of the key reasons for this extra hour is to allow for security checks to be conducted.

    Below are some of the safety measures in place:

    1. Participation will be capped at 7,000 to reduce congestion
    2. Taller and bigger cones will be used for better visibility on the route
    3. Route marshals will be equipped with light sticks for better visibility
    4. More paramedics and route marshals will be deployed
    5. The event’s emergency hotline number will be printed on every participant’s bib, helmet stickers and bike tag for reference
    6. Safety signs will be prominently displayed along the route
    7. Selfie Points will be designated on the route for photo-taking
    8. Additional security checks will be carried out

    To prevent any security compromises or potential threats, we cannot divulge the full details of our security plan to the public. We are working with the relevant authorities to implement all the necessary safety and security measures. Participants are encouraged to stay hydrated at all times before, during and after your rides. There will be hydration stations located at the start point, on the route as well as the finish point.
